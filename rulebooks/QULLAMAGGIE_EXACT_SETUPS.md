## SETUP 1: BREAKOUTS

### What is a Breakout?
A stock that has made a big move higher (30-100%+) over 1-3 months, pulled back into a tight consolidation, then breaks out of that range expansion.

### The 3 Steps to the Setup

**Step 1: Big Initial Move**
```
- Stock up 30-100%+ in past 1-3 months
- Move can last few days to few weeks
- This establishes the trend
```

**Step 2: Orderly Pullback & Consolidation**
```
- Price pulls back from the high
- Forms consolidation (tightening range)
- Higher lows pattern develops
- Consolidation duration: 2 weeks to 2 months
- Stock price "surfs" the 10-day MA and 20-day MA
- Sometimes also respects 50-day MA
- This is the setup formation phase
```

**Step 3: Range Expansion (The Breakout)**
```
- Price breaks out of consolidation range
- Confirmation: Strong volume on breakout
- This is the entry signal
```

### How to Trade Breakouts

**Before Market Open:**
```
1. Have a watchlist prepared
2. Identify potential breakout candidates
3. Set price alerts
4. Pre-decide how many shares you want to buy
```

**Entry - Opening Range High (ORH):**
```
Choose timeframe for ORH:
  • 1-minute candle high
  • 5-minute candle high
  • 60-minute candle high (first candle is 9:30-10:00 AM EST)
  • Can use combination or just daily chart entry

You don't have to use intraday charts.
Just look at daily and enter when starting to break out.

Method: Anticipating breakout is less effective.
Better: Enter when breakout is actually happening.
```

**Stop Loss - Always Low of Day:**
```
Stop placement = Low of the current trading day

CRITICAL RULE: Stop should NOT be wider than ADR or ATR

Example:
  ADR = 5%  → Stop max 5% away from entry
  ADR = 3%  → Stop max 3% away from entry
```

**Profit Management - Scale Out:**
```
After 3-5 days in the trade:
  • Sell 1/3 to 1/2 of position if the trade has worked
  • Lock in gains from initial move
  
Remaining 1/2 to 2/3:
  • Move stop loss to break even (protect from loss)
  • Trail with 10-day MA (beginners) or 20-day MA (experienced)
  • Exit on first CLOSE BELOW the MA
  • Wait for complete candle close below MA, not just intraday touch
```

**Asymmetric Upside:**
```
In bullish markets:
  • Large upside can come from holding the remainder while momentum continues
  • This is why the setup works so well
```

### How to Find Breakout Setups

**Daily Scan:**
```
Scan for top 1-2% biggest gainers over these timeframes:
  1. 1-month performance
  2. 3-month performance
  3. 6-month performance

This shows which stocks are LEADING right now.

These are the candidates most likely to break out.
```

### Key Insights

- Works on daily chart primarily (for swing trading)
- Also works on weekly chart (longer timeframe)
- Also works on intraday 1-min and 5-min charts (called "Holy Grail" by some)
- Can also be called "high tight flags" or triangles
- Pattern: Stocks in stair-step moves (20-50%+ move, pullback, consolidate, then repeat)

## SETUP 2: EPISODIC PIVOT (EP)

### What is an Episodic Pivot?
When unexpected GOOD news hits a stock (especially neglected ones), it can trigger multi-month or multi-year moves.

### Types of Episodic Pivots
```
1. Earnings and earnings guidance surprises
2. Government regulations, political news
3. Biotech: Drug trial results, FDA decisions
4. Macro and political events
5. ... many more

Primary focus: Earnings and earnings guidance
These tend to have the best results.
```

### The 4 Steps to the Setup

**Step 1: Gap Up 10%+**
```
Stock must gap up at least 10% from previous close
- Bigger gaps are better
- This is a hard requirement
```

**Step 2: Big Volume**
```
Volume requirement is CRITICAL:
  • If volume already in premarket: Good sign
  • If NOT in premarket: Must come at open
  • Best setups trade average daily volume in first 15-30 minutes after open
  • Example: Stock avg 500K shares/day → Trades 500K+ in first 30 minutes
  
This volume is the confirmation signal.
```

**Step 3: Big Growth Numbers (Earnings EPs)**
```
If earnings-related EP, need:
  • Big growth numbers
  • Preferably mid-digit, high-digit, or triple-digit growth
  • EPS growth: Often 100%+ above analyst expectations
  • Revenue growth: Often significant beat
  • Example: EPS 85¢ vs 67¢ expected = significant beat
  
This attracts institutional money and drives multi-month moves.
```

**Step 4: Stock NOT Already in Uptrend**
```
Best EPs come from:
  • Stocks in downtrend or consolidation (last 3-6 months)
  • NOT stocks that already made big 50-100%+ move into the gap-up
  
Why? If stock already rallied, gap-up less likely to be a surprise.
The market is already aware.

Best EPs: Neglected stocks that suddenly surprise everyone.
```

### How to Trade Episodic Pivots

**Identification (After-hours or Pre-market):**
```
1. Watch earnings reports after market close
2. Or pre-market stock movers before open
3. Identify gaps of 10%+ on big volume
4. Check if growth numbers are significant
5. Verify stock wasn't already in major uptrend
```

**Entry - Opening Range High (ORH):**
```
Same as Breakout setup:
  • 1-minute candle high
  • 5-minute candle high
  • 60-minute candle high
  
Wait for stock to start moving higher at open.
```

**Stop Loss:**
```
Stop at lows of the day (same as breakouts)
Note: Lows may not be established immediately for gap-ups.
```

**Exit:**
```
Trail stop with:
  • 10-day MA (conservative)
  • 20-day MA (more aggressive)
  
Exit on first CLOSE BELOW the MA.
Create your own sell rules based on what works for you.
```

### Learning Curve

```
Time to mastery: 4-8 earnings seasons
            = 1-2 years of trading these

Why? EPs are more emotional and newsflow-driven.
Takes time to understand market reactions to different types of news.

Once you learn it: Very little work to find them.
They basically "come to you" during earnings season.
```

### Historical Examples Provided

- **NVDA 2016-2017**: Massive earnings beat → 126% EPS growth, 48% revenue growth
  After gap-up, stock nearly doubled over following months

- **FSLR 2007**: Earnings nearly tripled, gap up with massive volume

- **BB (BBRY) 2004**: Earnings surprise → Multi-month move

## SETUP 3: PARABOLIC SHORT (or Long)

### What is a Parabolic Move?
A stock that has extended extremely far, extremely fast. Think of it like a stretched rubber band ready to snap back.

### The 2 Steps to the Setup

**Step 1: Extreme Move**
```
Stock up 50-100%+ in few days or weeks (if larger cap)
        up 300-1000%+ in few days or weeks (if smaller cap)
```

**Step 2: Acceleration**
```
Stock should be up 3-5+ days in a row
Many times you see:
  • Weeks or months of steady trend
  • Then starts speeding up significantly
  • Some explode from nowhere
```

### How to Trade Parabolic Shorts

**Entry Options:**
```
1. Short on opening range lows (1-min, 5-min candles)
2. Wait for first red 5-minute candle (if stock gaps straight up)
3. Or wait for bounce into VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)
   - If fails at VWAP, that's your short entry
   - Entry on first red 1-min or 5-min candle after VWAP fail

Key: Get relatively tight and defined stops.
```

**Stop Loss:**
```
Stop at highs of the day (opposite of longs)
OR if using VWAP: Stop at reclaim of VWAP
```

**Target:**
```
Target area: 10-day and 20-day moving averages
This is where parabolic stocks typically bounce/consolidate
```

### Important Rules

**Don't Be Early:**
```
WAIT for the right setup.
Let the amateur shorts get run over.
YOUR EDGE comes from waiting for perfect setup.
```

**Upside Difference:**
```
Breakout/EP setups: large open-ended upside is possible
Parabolic shorts: usually more limited upside

But: Better win rate if you wait for perfect setup
     You can be "too early" and get destroyed
```

**Parabolic Long (Rare)**
```
Sometimes after parabolic short crashes 50-60%+ in few days:
  Can bounce 50-100% in few days (long setup)

Entry: Opening range highs or first green 5-min candle
       Any range break to upside

These are less common than short setups.
```

### Key Insight

```
"Think of stocks as rubber bands.
 If they get really stretched short term,
 they can have powerful snapbacks."

This is by far the RISKIEST setup if done wrong.
Or if you have issues obeying your stops.

Most are short setups. Long setups less common.
```
